## 2. Context
The Trips bounded context manages collaborative travel planning.
It is responsible for:
*   Trip lifecycle and status transitions.
*   Destinations, titles, descriptions, and cover image metadata.
*   Travel schedules (start and end dates).
*   Trip participant lists.
*   Organizers.
*   Timeline logs of trip actions.

It is **not** responsible for:
*   Group membership lists.
*   User authentication.
*   Shared expenses.
*   Settlements.
*   Notifications.

---

## 3. Aggregate Boundary

### Aggregate Root: `Trip`

The `Trip` aggregate contains:
*   **Metadata:** Title, Description, Destination, Cover Image.
*   **Schedule:** Start Date, End Date.
*   **Organizer:** Organizer User ID.
*   **Settings:** Visibility, Allow Invites, Archived.
*   **Status:** PLANNING, ACTIVE, COMPLETED, CANCELLED.

### Aggregate Ownership
*   **Trip Aggregate Owns:**
    *   `Trip`
    *   `TripParticipant`
    *   `TripActivity`
*   **Trip Aggregate References:**
    *   `Group` (`groupId` only)
    *   `User` (`organizerId` and participant `userId`s only)
*   **Trip Aggregate Does Not Own:**
    *   Group membership
    *   Expenses
    *   Settlements
    *   Notifications

### Aggregate Consistency Boundary
*   All modifications to `TripParticipant` and `TripActivity` must occur through the `Trip` aggregate root.
*   No external bounded context may directly create, modify, or delete `TripParticipant` or `TripActivity` records.
*   All invariant enforcement is performed inside the `Trip` aggregate.

---

## 4. Supporting Entities

*   **TripParticipant:** Tracks trip-specific participant status. Refers to the parent Group member.
*   **TripActivity:** Immutable log of trip lifecycle and participant events.

These are supporting entities encapsulated within the `Trip` aggregate boundary and accessed only through the `Trip` root.

---

## 5. External References & Identifier Policy

### External References
The `Trip` aggregate references external entities by ID only:
*   `groupId`: Links to the parent Group.
*   `organizerId`: Links to the organizer User.
*   `userId`: Inside participants, links to User.

We do **not** embed `Group` or `User` JPA entities inside the Trip database schema, keeping database models decoupled.

### Identifier Policy
*   Identifiers (`tripId`, `tripParticipantId`, `tripActivityId`) are UUIDs generated by the application.
*   External aggregates are referenced only through UUID identifiers.

---

## 6. Concurrency Strategy
*   Mutable aggregates use optimistic locking (`@Version`).
*   Concurrent updates that violate version checks must fail and be retried by the client.
*   Supporting entities participate in aggregate consistency and must never circumvent optimistic locking guarantees.

---

## 7. Transaction Boundary

Each command operation executes within a single transactional boundary.

All modifications to the Trip aggregate, including TripParticipant and
TripActivity changes, are committed atomically.

Cross-bounded-context operations are never part of the same database
transaction.

---

## 8. Deletion Policy
*   Trips are soft deleted (`is_deleted`, `deleted_at`, `deleted_by`).
*   TripActivity records are immutable historical records and remain preserved after Trip soft deletion.
*   Physical deletion is not part of the application lifecycle.

---

## 9. Cross-Context Interaction Rules

### Communication Rule
Bounded contexts must communicate through published service interfaces (queries, facade services, or events), never by directly accessing another context's JPA repositories or database tables.

*   **Allowed queries from Trips to Groups:**
    *   Is parent group active?
    *   Is user a member of the group?
    *   Is user owner or admin of the group?
*   **Forbidden operations from Trips to Groups:**
    *   Adding group members.
    *   Modifying group roles or transferring ownership.
    *   Archiving or deleting groups.

---

## 10. Core Invariants

*   **Single Organizer:** Every Trip has exactly one organizer.
*   **Organizer Participation:** The organizer must always remain an active participant in the Trip.
*   **Group Membership Scope:** Trip participants must already be active members of the parent Group.
*   **Schedule Ordering:** `startDate <= endDate` must always hold true.
*   **Group Status Check:** Trips cannot be created inside archived or deleted Groups.
*   **Completion Lock:** Completed trips become read-only.
*   **Parent Relationship:** A Trip cannot exist without its parent Group reference.

---

## 11. Lifecycle Transitions & Behavioral Rules

### Transitions Diagram
The allowed status transitions are:

```text
PLANNING ──► ACTIVE ──► COMPLETED
   │
   └──► CANCELLED
```

No other transitions are allowed.

### Behavioral Rules
*   **PLANNING:**
    *   Editable.
    *   Participants may join or leave.
    *   Organizer may update schedule dates.
*   **ACTIVE:**
    *   Schedule becomes fixed.
    *   Participants remain manageable.
*   **COMPLETED:**
    *   Read-only.
    *   No participant modifications allowed.
*   **CANCELLED:**
    *   Read-only.
    *   Historical reference only.

---

## 12. CQRS-lite & Read Models

### Command Operations
*   `TripCommandService`
*   `ParticipantCommandService`

### Query Operations
*   `TripQueryService`
*   `TimelineQueryService`

### Read Models Policy
*   `TripQueryService` exposes DTO projections only.
*   JPA entities are never returned by REST controllers.
*   Dashboard-specific DTOs may aggregate data from multiple repositories.

---

## 13. Domain Events & Timing

### Events Suite
*   `TripCreatedEvent`
*   `TripUpdatedEvent`
*   `TripStartedEvent`
*   `TripCompletedEvent`
*   `TripCancelledEvent`
*   `ParticipantInvitedEvent`
*   `ParticipantJoinedEvent`
*   `ParticipantRemovedEvent`

### Event Timing Rules
*   Domain events are published from transactional command services after successful aggregate persistence.
*   Events are immutable and represent facts that have already occurred.
*   Events must never be published from query services.

---

## 14. Non-Goals
*   Tracking expense ledger allocations (Trip does not own Expenses).
*   Managing debt settlements.
*   Providing travel analytics or dashboards.
*   Triggering external notifications.

---

## 15. Future Sprints Dependencies

*   **Sprint 06 (Shared Expenses):** Integrates by storing a foreign key relationship referencing `TripId`. Expenses will reference Trip, but Trip will never maintain financial totals.
*   **Sprint 07 (Settlements):** Integrates by checking `TripId`.
*   **Sprint 08 (Analytics):** Consumes `TripActivity` events.
*   **Sprint 10 (Notifications):** Consumes domain events listed in Section 13.
